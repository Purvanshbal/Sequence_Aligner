About:

Welcome to the CS 466 Final Project: Sequence Aligner! Our team, consisting of Pooja Ganesh, Dirgh Shah, and Purvansh Bal, is excited to present this interactive web platform designed to implement and visualize various sequence alignment algorithms. From Global Alignment to Local Alignment, Fitting Alignment, Gap Affine Penalty Alignment, and Multiple Sequence Alignment, our tool caters to DNA, RNA, and protein sequences. Our approach involves leveraging class-covered algorithms, utilizing standard visualization techniques, and implementing the project in Python with the Django framework. With a planned timeline encompassing algorithm implementation, frontend enhancements, and visualization features, we strive to create a comprehensive tool. Regular meetings and hack sessions, coupled with version control through GitHub, ensure effective collaboration. Inspired by the aligner provided in class (https://valiec.github.io/AlignmentVisualizer/index.html), our project aims to offer a robust and user-friendly experience in the field of sequence alignment. Explore the diverse functionalities of our aligner, and stay tuned for updates as we progress through the development phases!.

How to Use:

Use the navigation bar at the top of the page to toggle between different aligners we offer.
